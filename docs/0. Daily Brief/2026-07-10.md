### Content of the Day — Tool Spotlight: NetExec (`nxc`)

NetExec is the maintained successor to CrackMapExec (CME went unmaintained around 2023), and it's the single tool you'll reach for most once you hold *any* valid credential material in a Windows/AD environment. It's protocol-oriented — `nxc smb`, `winrm`, `ldap`, `mssql`, `ssh`, `ftp`, `rdp`, `wmi`, `nfs` — and its whole job is answering two questions fast: *where does this credential work across the network, and what can I pull with it?* That's the pivot from "I popped one box" to "I own the domain," which is exactly the shape of every OSCP AD set and CPTS AD lab.

The core loop is spray-and-map. `nxc smb 10.10.10.0/24 -u users.txt -p 'Autumn2026!' --continue-on-success` walks a subnet and marks every host where the cred authenticates; the magic string is **`(Pwn3d!)`**, which means that principal is local admin there. Pass-the-hash is a drop-in: swap `-p` for `-H <NTLM>` (T1550.002). For unauthenticated recon it does null (`-u '' -p ''`) and guest sessions, plus `--shares`, `--users`, `--rid-brute`, `--pass-pol`, `--loggedon-users`, and `--sessions`. On the LDAP protocol you get `--kerberoasting out.txt` and `--asreproast out.txt` straight out of the box (T1558.003 / T1558.004), and `--bloodhound --collection All` to feed the graph without dropping SharpHound on disk.

Where it earns its keep is loot once you have admin. `--sam` and `--lsa` pull local secrets (T1003.002); `--ntds` performs a drsuapi-based NTDS.dit dump — effectively DCSync against a DC (T1003.006). The module system (`-L` to list) extends this: `-M lsassy` remotely dumps and parses LSASS across a host list (T1003.001), `-M spider_plus` recursively spiders shares to JSON so you can grep for `web.config`, `unattend.xml`, or KeePass DBs, and `-M gpp_password` / `-M gpp_autologin` decrypt cPassword out of SYSVOL. Kerberos-only paths (no NTLM) work with `-k` / `--use-kcache` once you've got a ccache exported. Reach for single-purpose Impacket scripts (secretsdump, GetUserSPNs) when you need surgical control or a weird edge case — but for sweeping a network and triaging access at scale, `nxc` is the default. You'll hit it constantly on HTB AD chains, PG practice boxes, and the PEN-200 AD lab.
